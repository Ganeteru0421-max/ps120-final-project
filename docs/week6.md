# Climate

 ##  Purpose

As part of this Earth Science course, you will complete a course-long project. You will research the geophysical processes of a country of your choice (for example, your birth country or a country for which you have close ties). Up through Week 6 of the block, you will work on different segments of this assignment and add high-quality, relevant content to a website that you will create. At the end of Week 6, you will have a completed website detailing the Earth’s internal and external geographic processes and how the physical geography of the region impacted the development of your country. As you complete this project you will develop soft skills that will be beneficial to your future career.

## Instructions

**PART A: WEBSITE BUILDING - DUE BY SATURDAY @ 11:59 PM!**

This week, we have discussed the atmosphere, severe weather, and climate change. For this next segment of the course-long assignment, you will be researching the following questions about your country and including information on your web page about the following:  

**Global Position and Weather Patterns**: 

-   How does your country’s location affect the following global weather systems: global circulation cells, global precipitation patterns, and wind belt systems? 

**Severe Weather**: 

-   What types of severe weather (e.g., hurricanes, tornadoes, floods, wildfires) are common in your country? Provide examples of these events. 

**Climate Classification**: 

-   What climate types are found in your country (use the Köppen Classification)? Briefly describe each climate type. Include a climate map for your country. 

**Biomes**: 

-   Which biomes (distinct ecosystems) exist in your country? Do they unite society or contribute to divisions? 

**Native Plants**: 

-   Are there any native plants that provide food, resources, or medicine? Are there any poisonous or dangerous plants? 

**Native Animals**: 

-   Are there any native animals that are useful for food, resources, farming, or transportation? Are any animals dangerous or poisonous? 

Include a video, podcast, interview, pictures, charts, or graphs to bring your research to life.

Make sure to cite your reputable sources, using proper APA formatting. (Note: Using artificial intelligence (AI) as a springboard for deeper research can be beneficial. However, it is a tool, not a substitution for good research. Direct copying and pasting from AI websites is unacceptable. Always back up your findings with high-quality sources, and be sure to cite your use of AI properly.) 

Update your website with this information.

In your discussion board post, state the name of the country you researched and include the link to your website. You will peer review **three** other students’ websites next week.
